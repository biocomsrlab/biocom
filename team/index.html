Engr. Saifur Rahaman

Assistant Professor, Department of Computer Science and Engineering
PhD Candidate, City University of Hong Kong, Kowloon, HK SAR

Former PhD Research scholar, Center for Systems Biology, Department of Radiation Oncology, Harvard Medical School
Formar Graduate Research Schoolar, Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard

Founder & Principal Investigator
BioCoM SR Lab – Laboratory for Bioinformatics and Computational Medicine

Saifur Rahaman is a researcher working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, computational biology, bioinformatics, and translational medicine, with a strong focus on developing next-generation computational frameworks for solving complex biomedical challenges. His research aims to bridge advanced computational intelligence with real-world clinical applications to accelerate scientific discovery and improve precision healthcare.

His primary research focuses on early cancer detection using liquid biopsy technologies, where he develops machine learning and AI-driven diagnostic systems for identifying ultra-early molecular signatures from circulating biomarkers, including cell-free DNA (cfDNA), circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA), cell-free RNA (cfRNA), methylation patterns, and multi-omics clinical data. His long-term vision is to build generalizable computational frameworks capable of enabling population-scale cancer screening and early disease interception.

Beyond cancer diagnostics, his research explores single-cell genomics, spatial transcriptomics, tumor microenvironment modeling, anticancer drug response prediction, explainable artificial intelligence (XAI), foundation AI models for biomedical data, and computational precision oncology. He is particularly interested in designing intelligent systems that integrate heterogeneous biological data to uncover disease mechanisms and support personalized therapeutic decision-making.

Saifur founded BioCoM SR Lab with the vision of establishing an interdisciplinary research environment where biology, medicine, and computational intelligence converge to develop transformative technologies for next-generation healthcare, precision medicine, and data-driven biomedical discovery.

His broader mission is to advance computational biomedical research that translates fundamental biological understanding into clinically impactful technologies capable of improving patient diagnosis, treatment, and long-term health outcomes worldwide.

Hongyoon Choi

Visiting Professor, Seoul National University Hospital

MD from Seoul National University College of Medicine

PhD from Seoul National University Graduate School of Convergence Science

Hongyoon Choi grew up in Seoul, South Korea, and obtained his M.D. from Seoul National University College of Medicine in 2010. He received the Korean Nuclear Medicine Board certification in 2015 after completing his residency at Seoul National University Hospital. His research primarily focuses on leveraging artificial intelligence in medical imaging and molecular medicine, with a recent emphasis on spatial biology data. He integrates diverse biological and medical data to discover biomarkers and disease targets through translational studies. By combining spatial transcriptomics with AI, he aims to initiate new theranostics and develop novel biomarkers. During his research, he co-founded Portrai, Inc., a biotech company transforming drug development using large-scale spatial transcriptomics data from human disease tissues. In his free time, he enjoys playing soccer, swimming, and Nintendo with his two young kids, as well as traveling.

Len W Katsumata

Visiting Scientist from Daiichi Sankyo Co., Ltd.

MS from the The University of Tokyo School of Science

PhD from the The University of Tokyo School of Science

Len is a visiting scientist from Daiichi Sankyo Co., Ltd., a global pharmaceutical company based in Tokyo, Japan. There he played a pivotal role in the translational research of various oncology pipeline projects, with a particular focus on proprietary antibody-drug-conjugates (ADCs). Born and raised in the suburbs of Philadelphia, Len moved to Japan after high school to pursue his university education and subsequently launched his career at his current company. During his doctoral studies, he investigated the heterogeneity of fibroblast populations in the mouse liver utilizing diverse disease models to elucidate their distinct roles in driving liver fibrosis. His current research interests lie in deciphering drug resistance mechanisms, specifically looking at spatial and inter-cellular communication within the tumor microenvironment of PDAC over the course of treatment. He seeks to contribute his unique industry expertise and experience to the lab, offering key insights that translate exploratory laboratory discoveries into meaningful clinical outcomes. Outside the lab, he enjoys spending active time with his two children at the park. Once the kids are in bed, he loves winding down with his wife, sharing snacks, a good glass of wine, and their favorite anime.
